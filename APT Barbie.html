APT Barbie

It's a beautiful clear blue morning sky, as a black Audi RS3 pulls into a shopping center parking lot. A female journalist’s voice crackles over the radio—“Law enforcement officials this morning are still refusing to release details regarding last month’s cyber attack against—” The engine and radio both fall silent to the sounds of birds chirping in the distance.

The driver’s door opens. A young blonde woman in her early twenties steps out, oversized sunglasses masking her expression. She moves with quiet confidence towards the entrance of a Sephora, her reflection briefly caught in the sliding glass door before she disappears inside.

Moments later, a Maryland State Police Ford Explorer rolls past, slow and deliberate. Behind it, an unmarked black Chevy Tahoe follows, its tinted windows revealing nothing. Its white Federal license plate with blue lettering lingers before vanishing from view.

Inside, she counts out crisp $50 bills, her tone cheerful, polite. The cashier hands her a bag, smiles and tells her to have a great day. She exits, stepping into the sunlight.

Suddenly—the sharp metallic clank of a pump-action shotgun racking a shell into its chamber.

"DON’T MOVE! STATE POLICE! SHOW ME YOUR FUCKING HANDS!"

She flinches—The shopping bag slips from her hand, hitting the pavement with a soft, hollow thud.

CUT TO BLACK

A muffled ding. The doors of an elevator slide open, and a man in an Army green uniform steps inside, followed by a younger man in a black suit. The uniformed man pulls a key from his pocket, inserts it into a lock on the panel, turns it and presses five. The elevator begins to descend.

"Sir, I have to ask... the FrostyGoop payload she—"

A sharp glance cuts him off.

"Kenneth, this isn't Langely. Everyone here at Meade gets paid to be quiet."

"Sorry, sir. It’s just—with the recent Chinese telco compromises—"

A pause. Then, a smile with a calm but final: "Son... This might be your only trip down here, so listen up. Roughly two years ago Uncle Sam caught her hand in the cookie jar, threw a black bag over her head and now she’s on a classified contract. But the most important thing?"

The elevator slows. A beat of silence.

"If we're not in a SCIF—we don’t discuss her operations."

DING. The doors slide open.

A vast cubicle farm stretches into the darkness—empty, windowless, lifeless, abandoned. The soft, haunting notes of Portishead’s "Roads" drift through the space, the melancholic melody echoing off the walls.

Kenneth hesitates, looking to the uniformed man, as he removes the elevator key and steps forward without pause, moving through the darkness like he’s done this a hundred times before. Kenneth follows. Their footsteps are the only sound aside from the music.

At the far end of the completely dark room, a faint neon pink glow at a lone workstation in the back corner provides the only light. A slight humming of monitors fills the space against the music. The pink light morphs like the flame of a candle against the edges of cubicle walls as they approach.

The song’s lyrics whisper through the air—

"Ohh, can't anybody see? We've got a war to fight... Never found our way... Regardless of what they say..."

They round the final corner.

A figure sits at a desk with a pink lava lamp, writing code, fully immersed in the glow of four monitors streaming lines of data, live attack feeds, and a world most people will never see. The person’s back is to them, a pink hoodie with cat ears pulled over their head, the glow of the screens casting a soft outline around them.

The music swells— The figure stops typing—

"From this moment... How can it feel, this wrong?"

The uniformed man doesn’t break stride. His voice cuts through the song, firm, sharp, unwavering.

"How many dag on times have I told you that this is a military installation, and you will dress appropriately?"

With the slight stroke of the mouse and a solitary click, the music cuts out.

A tiny, faint meow breaks the silence.

The chair slowly turns.

The blonde woman from the parking lot sits before them, legs crossed, expression bored, idly stroking a tiny kitten curled in her lap. She lets the moment linger before rolling her eyes, mock-dramatic.

"I'm still wearing the ankle bracelet. That should be enough for you."

The uniformed man exhales slowly, closing his eyes briefly before opening them and turning to Kenneth.

"Special Agent Kenneth Dahl, this is Barbara. She's one of our black hat hackers. Some Neanderthals around here like to call her APT Barbie."

Barbara rolls her eyes again, this time showing actual contempt for the moniker. Kenneth, eager, steps forward, hand extended for a handshake.

Barbara doesn’t move. Instead, she scoffs, still petting her kitten, then tilts her head slightly towards the uniformed man as if considering something before responding—half-singing, half-mocking, quoting Eminem:

"But what if he’s right and I’m just a criminal? Making a living off of the world’s misery... what in the world gives me the right?"

The uniformed man clears his throat—a sharp, intentional cough.

Barbara smirks but says nothing more, her fingers tracing slow circles along the kitten’s fur.

Kenneth slowly lowers his hand and innocently asks "So, uh, whats your cat's name?""

Barbara's smirk grows wide as she looks Kenneth directly in the eyes and simply replies...

"Her name is Earned Privilege."

Kenneth lets a slight chuckle escape as Barbara's eyes and attention quickly shift down lovingly to her kitten.

"...but Mommy calls her Privs."

END SCENE
